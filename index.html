Customized Meal Plan Automation for Restaurant


- The plan was to automate the process of generating customized meal plans based on specific dietary requirements, such as calorie counts or dietary restrictions.
- Users can now interact with the system by requesting meal plans that fit their needs. For instance, they might ask for a meal plan that provides 400 calories, is vegetarian, or excludes gluten.
- To address this, we utilized Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system. By Ingesting menu cards in vector databases, and after that when user sends the query, It gets the relevant items from the menu and then we combined the retrieval with query and passed it LLM which is properly engineered w.r.t prompt. This setup allows to generate customized meal plans with items that were in menu.
- The integration of LLM and RAG technology automates the creation of meal plans, providing users with personalized recommendations swiftly and accurately, enhancing the overall dining experience.

Human Face Detection and Recognition


- MTCNN (Multi-Task Cascaded Convolutional Networks): I used MTCNN for face detection. MTCNN is adept at detecting faces in an image and providing bounding boxes around them.
- ResNet (Residual Network): This is often used for the task of feature extraction. In the context of facial recognition, you can use a pre-trained ResNet model to obtain facial encodings or embeddings. These encodings are numerical representations of the facial features that can be used for recognition.
- Euclidean Distance (L2 Norm): After all I used Euclidean distance metric to compare the embeddings of a newly detected face with images of authorized individuals stored in a designated folder. The set threshold was 0.7, indicating that if the similarity between the new person's face and any existing authorized image exceeded 70%, the individual would be granted authorization, and their name would be recorded accordingly.
